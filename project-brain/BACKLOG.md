## Research-Driven Opportunities (2026-04-03 Research Cycle)

### P1: QuickBooks Integration
- Every major competitor has it (Jobber, HCP, ServiceTitan)
- Users expect it — QB sync errors are Jobber's #1 complaint, meaning the bar is low
- Impact: Blocking objection for many contractors evaluating tools
- Source: RESEARCH.md competitive analysis

### P1: AI-Powered Estimate Generation
- 38% of contractors already using AI, gap doubling yearly
- Handoff.ai, QuoteGoat showing the way — upload photos → get estimate
- We have unique subdivision data that could feed AI estimating (avg home size, year built, urgency)
- Impact: Major differentiator if we ship first among FSM platforms
- Source: [Marketing Code](https://www.marketingcode.com/contractor-ai-adoption-doubled-industry-shift/)

### P2: Automated Text/SMS Reminders
- Jobber, HCP, ServiceTitan all offer this
- Contractors expect appointment reminders, follow-up texts
- Could use Twilio or Resend (they also do SMS now)
- Impact: Reduces no-shows, improves client experience

### P2: Online Booking / Service Request Portal
- Jobber and HCP both let homeowners book services directly
- We have the client portal infrastructure — could add booking
- Impact: Reduces phone calls for contractors, captures leads

### P2: ACH Payment Option on Invoices
- ACH costs $5 max vs $29+ for credit card on $1,000 invoice
- Stripe supports ACH Direct Debit with same API
- Impact: Saves contractors hundreds/month in processing fees
- Source: [Stripe ACH docs](https://docs.stripe.com/invoicing/ach-direct-debit)

### P2: Interactive Onboarding Flow
- SaaS companies with guided onboarding see faster time-to-value and lower churn
- New users currently land on empty dashboard with no guidance
- Impact: Directly reduces early churn — highest-leverage investment per research
- Source: [Arcade SaaS marketing](https://www.arcade.software/post/saas-marketing-strategy)

### P3: Expand Data Coverage to Barrow, Hall, Douglas Counties
- Atlanta housing permits migrating to these emerging counties
- Gwinnett contracting (29% decline) but premium housing doubling
- Forsyth: New 140-acre mixed-use development (The Crossing at Coal Mountain) opening 2026
- Impact: More data = more opportunities for contractors
- Source: [HBWeekly](https://blog.hbweekly.com/metro-atlanta-new-residential-construction-a-2025-market-overview/)

### P3: SEO Landing Pages Per Metro
- SEO is highest-ROI acquisition channel (CAC $205, 60% below paid)
- Create pages: "HVAC contractor software Atlanta", "Roofing leads Forsyth County"
- Impact: Organic acquisition at scale
- Source: [Powered by Search](https://www.poweredbysearch.com/learn/saas-growth-strategy/)

### P4: Referral Program
- Turn satisfied contractors into acquisition channels
- $100-500 credit for successful referrals (industry standard)
- Impact: Lowest CAC channel after SEO
