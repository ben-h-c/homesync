## 1. FORSYTH COUNTY DEMOGRAPHICS & ECONOMICS

### Population
- **2026 estimated population**: 286,813 (1.42% annual growth)
- **2010 population**: 176,736 → **62% growth since 2010**
- **Fastest-growing county in Georgia**, 15th fastest nationally (2010-2019)
- **13th wealthiest county in the nation** (Forbes)
- **8th most populated county in Georgia** (out of 159)

### Income & Wealth
- **Median household income**: $143,784 (2024) — far above national median of ~$75K
- **Poverty rate**: 4.34% (vs national 12.5%)
- **8,000+ businesses** in the county

### Demographics (for marketing & name diversity)
- 63.5% White
- 19.7% Asian
- 4.3% Black/African American
- ~10% Hispanic (various census measures)
- Remaining: multiracial, other

### Housing Stock
- **~155,000 housing units** total (primarily single-family)
- **~30% of homes built in the last 20 years** (massive construction wave 2000-2020)
- Construction peaked during 2004-2008 and 2012-2018 cycles

### Property Tax
- **County millage rate**: 7.896 mills (held steady 5 consecutive years)
- **School millage**: 15.208 mills operations + 1.418 mills bonds
- **Total effective tax rate**: ~1.18% (above national median of 1.02%)
- **Assessment ratio**: 40% of fair market value (Georgia statewide standard)
- Example: $500,000 market value → $200,000 assessed → ~$5,523 annual tax

### Schools (Key Home Value Driver)
- **Forsyth County Schools**: Ranked **#3 in Georgia** (Niche 2025)
- **Top-ranked district among large districts in the state**
- Alliance Academy for Innovation: #2 high school in GA (100% graduation rate)
- Lambert High: #7 in state; South Forsyth High: #12
- Daves Creek Elementary: #5 in state
- School quality is the **#1 driver** of home values and family relocation to the area

### GA-400 Highway Expansion
- **$4.6 billion** express lanes project (largest highway project in Georgia history)
- 16-mile stretch from North Springs MARTA station to McFarland Pkwy in Forsyth
- **No public funding** — privately financed ($4.05B concession payment to state)
- Heavy construction starts **July 2026**
- Completion expected **2031**
- Will add 2 managed express lanes in each direction
- **Impact**: Short-term construction disruption, long-term property value boost

## 4. SYSTEM LIFESPANS — ADJUSTED FOR GEORGIA CLIMATE

The Southeast climate (hot, humid summers; mild winters; high UV) **shortens lifespans** compared to national averages:

| System | National Avg | **Georgia Adjusted** | Key Climate Factor |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| HVAC (heat pump) | 15-20 years | **10-15 years** | Year-round heavy use; humidity forces extra moisture removal |
| HVAC (central AC + furnace) | 15-20 years | **12-17 years** | Compressor stress from long cooling seasons |
| Water Heater (tank) | 10-12 years | **8-12 years** | Hard water in some areas; high demand |
| Roof (3-tab shingle) | 20-25 years | **15-20 years** | UV degradation; thermal cycling; humidity weakens seals |
| Roof (architectural shingle) | 25-30 years | **20-25 years** | Better quality offsets some climate effect |
| Exterior Paint | 7-10 years | **5-8 years** | UV exposure; humidity/rain; temperature swings |
| Driveway (asphalt) | 15-20 years | **10-15 years** | Heat softening; UV |
| Deck Stain/Seal | 3-5 years | **2-4 years** | Rain, humidity, UV |

**Key insight for the business**: Georgia's hotter climate means systems fail **sooner** than homeowners expect based on manufacturer warranties, creating an education opportunity and urgency.
